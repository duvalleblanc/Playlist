Westside Gunn - And Then You Pray For Me (2023)

My Thoughts

It's always great to see how an artist can consistently deliver a product you enjoy but it still is served fresh.

Westside Gunn is my favorite musical artist. He has consistently dropped albums that I enjoy and has always taken chances on music.

This new album is definitely a new approach to the modern boom bap, minimalistic production he usually works with. This new

album showcases a new addition to the typical Griselda sound, Trap beats and 808 drums. This album starts off with honestly is one

of these greatest Westside Gunn verses I have ever heard. He definitely wanted to make a statement from the begininning with the Mamas

Primetime verse. This is definitely my favroite song on this album and it tooks me about 20-30 minutes to actually move on to the next song

on the album. I think this album is definitely going to cause some issues with the fans who only prefer boom bap production. This album

will take a few more listens from people to get what he is doing with the blend of boom bap with 808 drums. The sound is still typical

Griselda Production but is just an addition of 808 drums and modern artists like J.I.D. and Ty Dolla $ign. I really love this album

and definitely think it is one of his best albums, not only in an artistic sense of creating a new canvas but re-inventing what has been done

already. The more you listen to this album the more you will enjoy it. This is definitely lives up to the name of being a Luxury audio experience.

The same way someone who was not into Westside Gunn and their ear (palette) for music at first told them that a Westside Gunn, Mach-Hommy,

MF DOOM, Brownsville Ka, or anyone else in this Undergrond Modern Boom Bap scene...you had to become accustomed to the sound. This is just a new canvas.

Roc Marciano - Marciology (2024)

My Thoughts

This album is another work of art from Masterclass Rapper/Producer, Roc Marciano.

The previous collab album with Producer/Rapper "The Alchemist", "The Elephant Man's Bones", in 2023, was

such a dark, menancing, heavy collection of songs that I was interested if Roc would continue wit this sound.

It was a pleasant surprise as going through this project that he went back to his soulful & minimalistic style of production but

with a touch of the dark spooky production. the intro song, Marciology, title track, pulls the listener into the dark abyss just like Rubber Hand Grip from

The Elephant Man's Bones album. The intro is almost as if he is conjuring up a spell of dark matter that the listener is taking part in

experiencing. The next song brings us back to the smooth minimal production with Goyard God. Lyrically this album is just such a continuous

onslaught of murderous rhymes and internal rhymes. The production in True Love and Roc's lyrics is just such a perfection marriage of the two.

By the time listener gets to Bebe's Kids it is just clearn that this guy is just on a different level. Roc is top 3 and my 2nd favorite Artist after Westside Gunn.

Larry June begininning Bad Juju was a great way to relax the listener and allow the album to rest with his more slower paced and laid back vocal deliver.

I don't want to review every single song so if you enjoy Roc Marciano and his music then this is a definitely MUST.
